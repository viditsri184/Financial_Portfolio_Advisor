SIP stands for Systematic Investment Plan, a disciplined method of investing in mutual funds.
Investors contribute a fixed amount at regular intervals such as monthly or quarterly.
It helps avoid market timing by averaging purchase cost through rupee-cost averaging.
SIP encourages consistent investment habits and reduces emotional decision-making.
Investing regularly ensures that even small amounts accumulate into significant corpus.
SIPs work best for long-term goals like retirement, education, or wealth creation.
Returns benefit from the power of compounding when investments are held for many years.
SIPs are suitable for all types of investors, including beginners.
Market volatility becomes less impactful when investing systematically over long periods.
SIPs offer flexibility to increase, decrease, pause, or stop contributions anytime.
