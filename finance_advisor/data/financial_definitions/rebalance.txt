Rebalancing means adjusting a portfolio back to its original asset allocation.
It restores the target risk level when market movements cause deviations.
Rebalancing may involve selling overperforming assets and buying underperforming ones.
It prevents the portfolio from becoming unintentionally aggressive or conservative.
Investors typically rebalance annually or semi-annually.
Rebalancing enforces discipline and reduces emotional decision-making.
It can improve long-term risk-adjusted returns without drastically affecting returns.
Rebalancing works best in diversified multi-asset portfolios.
Ignoring rebalancing can lead to excess risk exposure during market booms.
It is an essential step in maintaining portfolio stability and suitability.
