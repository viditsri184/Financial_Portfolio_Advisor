Asset allocation is the process of distributing investments across asset classes such as equity, debt, and gold.
It defines the risk-return characteristics of a portfolio more than individual fund selection.
Strategic allocation aligns with long-term goals, while tactical allocation adjusts to short-term opportunities.
A higher equity allocation increases growth potential but also increases volatility.
Debt allocation provides stability, income, and downside protection.
Gold helps hedge market volatility and acts as a safe-haven asset.
Balanced asset allocation improves consistency and reduces portfolio risk.
Rebalancing is required periodically to maintain target allocation.
