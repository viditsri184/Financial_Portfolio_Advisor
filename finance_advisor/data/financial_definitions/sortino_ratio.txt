The Sortino Ratio measures risk-adjusted return while considering only downside volatility.
It differentiates between harmful volatility and normal price fluctuations.
A higher Sortino Ratio indicates better protection against downside risk.
It is calculated using negative deviation instead of full standard deviation.
Sortino is more suitable than Sharpe for investors who prioritize capital protection.
Funds with high Sortino ratios tend to perform consistently in volatile markets.
Sortino helps identify portfolios that limit losses during downturns.
Investors use it along with maximum drawdown to assess defensive strength.
It is especially relevant for retirement and low-risk investment strategies.
Sortino Ratio provides a more realistic measure of real-world risk.
