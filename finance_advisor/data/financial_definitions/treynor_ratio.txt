Treynor Ratio evaluates risk-adjusted return using beta as the measure of risk.
It compares portfolio performance relative to systematic market risk.
A higher Treynor Ratio indicates better compensation for market-related risk.
It works best when comparing diversified portfolios where unsystematic risk is minimized.
Treynor Ratio is ideal for evaluating actively managed funds.
It differentiates managers who generate high returns through market exposure versus skill.
Beta-based risk measurement helps align portfolio evaluation with market dynamics.
Treynor should be used alongside Sharpe and alpha for a complete assessment.
It is widely used in performance attribution studies.
Treynor Ratio is valuable for portfolios aligned strongly to equity markets.
