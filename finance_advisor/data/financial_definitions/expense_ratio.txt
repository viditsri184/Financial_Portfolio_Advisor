The expense ratio represents the annual fee that mutual funds charge for managing investor money.
It includes management fees, administrative costs, audit fees, and distribution expenses.
A lower expense ratio generally improves long-term returns due to reduced cost drag.
Actively managed funds often have higher expense ratios than passive index funds.
Regulators like SEBI set limits on maximum allowable expense ratios to protect investors.
