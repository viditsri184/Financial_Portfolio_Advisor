Drawdown measures the decline from a peak value of an investment to its lowest point before recovery.
It represents potential downside risk and shows how much an investor could lose in worst periods.
A large drawdown indicates high vulnerability during market crashes or corrections.
Drawdown is crucial for retirement planning, risk profiling, and capital protection.
Investors should evaluate both magnitude and duration of drawdowns.
Funds with smaller and shorter drawdowns are more resilient during turbulent markets.
Portfolio diversification can reduce drawdowns by balancing asset movements.
Drawdown analysis is central to risk management and financial stress testing.
Monte Carlo simulations often incorporate drawdown probabilities and depth.
Understanding drawdown helps set realistic expectations during market downturns.
