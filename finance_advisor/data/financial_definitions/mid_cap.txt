Mid-cap companies occupy the middle ground between large-caps and small-caps in terms of size and stability.
They offer higher growth potential than large-caps but with moderately higher risk.
Mid-cap funds suit investors with medium to long-term horizons seeking accelerated growth.
These companies may be expanding, innovative, or gaining market share.
Mid-caps tend to outperform large-caps during strong market cycles.
However, they may be more volatile during economic slowdowns.
Regulators define mid-caps as companies ranked 101–250 by market capitalization.
Mid-caps add diversification and balance between safety and aggressive growth.
Investors should hold mid-caps for at least 5–7 years to absorb volatility.
Mid-cap allocation enhances overall portfolio return potential.
