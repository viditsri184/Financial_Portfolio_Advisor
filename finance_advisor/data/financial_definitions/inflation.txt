Inflation refers to the general increase in the prices of goods and services over time.
As inflation rises, the purchasing power of money decreases.
Investors must aim for inflation-adjusted (real) returns to preserve wealth.
Equities typically outperform inflation in the long run, while cash and deposits often lag.
High inflation reduces the real value of fixed-income returns and savings.
Central banks use monetary tools to control inflation and maintain economic stability.
Inflation impacts interest rates, bond yields, and consumer spending.
Long-term financial planning must include inflation assumptions for accuracy.
Investments like real estate, equities, and inflation-indexed bonds help hedge inflation.
Understanding inflation is crucial for setting realistic financial goals.
