A benchmark is a standard index used to evaluate the performance of a fund or portfolio.
Common benchmarks include NIFTY 50, SENSEX, NIFTY Midcap 150, and sectoral indices.
Benchmarks represent market performance for specific segments or asset classes.
A fund’s returns are compared against its benchmark to determine outperformance or underperformance.
Tracking error indicates how closely a fund follows its benchmark.
Passive index funds aim to replicate benchmark performance with minimal deviation.
Active funds strive to outperform the benchmark through stock selection.
Benchmark selection must match the fund’s investment objective and category.
Investors use benchmarks to assess fund manager skill and portfolio efficiency.
Understanding benchmarks is essential for evaluating investment performance.
