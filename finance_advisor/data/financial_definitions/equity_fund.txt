An equity fund primarily invests in shares of companies across various sectors and market caps.
These funds aim for long-term capital appreciation through ownership of businesses.
Equity funds can be categorized as large-cap, mid-cap, small-cap, multi-cap, or sectoral/thematic.
Returns are market-linked and may fluctuate based on economic cycles, company earnings, and global trends.
Equity funds suit investors with medium to long investment horizons willing to take higher risk.
They offer potential for higher returns compared to debt or hybrid funds.
Diversification across sectors and companies helps manage individual stock risk.
Regulators like SEBI mandate asset allocation guidelines for consistency and transparency.
Equity funds are ideal for wealth creation, retirement planning, and long-term goals.
Historical data shows equities outperform most asset classes over long periods despite volatility.
