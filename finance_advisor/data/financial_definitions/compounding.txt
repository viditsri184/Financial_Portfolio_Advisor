Compounding is the process by which investment earnings generate additional earnings when reinvested.
Over time, this snowball effect leads to exponential growth rather than linear growth.
The longer the investment horizon, the stronger the effect of compounding.
Compounding works best with consistent contributions and long-term discipline.
Even small amounts invested regularly can grow significantly due to compound returns.
SIPs in mutual funds leverage compounding effectively when started early.
Compounding requires patience, as its biggest benefits appear after many years.
Interruptions such as early withdrawals reduce compounding momentum.
Albert Einstein referred to compounding as the eighth wonder of the world because of its power.
Understanding compounding is essential for achieving retirement and wealth-building goals.
